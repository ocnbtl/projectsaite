## Continuity hierarchy

1. Identity and proportion accuracy.
2. Skin tone, facial structure, eye placement, hairline, and distinguishing features as observed in the selected evidence-linked sources.
3. Body proportion, height relationship to the set, and gait.
4. Hair state and motion.
5. Wardrobe silhouette and material.
6. Pose/expression nuance.
7. Environment and lighting style.

Lower priorities may never be improved by allowing higher-priority drift.

## Required sheet set

### CHR-SAGE-FACE-001 — face identity master

- Straight-on, left/right three-quarter, and left/right profile.
- Neutral expression; consistent hair, neutral background, lens family, camera distance, crop, light, and exposure.
- No beauty-effect reshaping, dramatic makeup, jewelry obstruction, wide-angle distortion, or mixed-age appearance.
- Purpose: establish identity control; not a public composite.

### CHR-SAGE-BODY-001 — proportion master

- Front, rear, left/right profile, and both three-quarter views.
- Neutral fitted unbranded clothing, neutral stance, flat floor plane.
- Consistent camera height, distance, focal length, and subject scale.
- Purpose: control body proportion, stance, leg/torso relationship, and set scale.

### CHR-SAGE-EXPR-001 — expression master

- Neutral, focused, confident runway, soft smile, warm smile, and glance-back connection.
- Facial geometry and apparent age remain unchanged.
- Purpose: constrain KF-01 through KF-06 emotional progression.

### CHR-SAGE-HAIR-001 — hair/movement master

- Resting; controlled backward motion; left-to-right; right-to-left; resort breeze; transition-safe silhouette.
- Hair volume, length, texture, hairline, and color remain source-controlled.
- Purpose: bridge walking continuity and hide the editorial transition without identity loss.

### CHR-SAGE-GAIT-001 — runway gait master

- Initial stance, contact, passing, mid-swing, opposite contact, near-camera stride.
- Same screen direction, shoe/foot geometry, stride character, camera axis, and floor plane.
- Purpose: define physically coherent approach beats.

### WARD-RUNWAY-001 — runway wardrobe turnaround

- Refined black architectural tailoring with controlled fabric movement.
- Front, rear, profiles, three-quarter views, hem/sleeve/fabric details.
- No logos, typography, recognizable trade dress, or unsafe near-lens hardware.
- Fabric must achieve 98–100% black-frame coverage during the hidden cut.

### WARD-RESORT-001 — resort wardrobe turnaround

- Refined ivory or warm-neutral linen-inspired look suitable for professional public portfolio use.
- Front, rear, profiles, three-quarter views, movement and fabric details.
- No logos, typography, or transparent/wardrobe-malfunction risk.
- Silhouette may transform across the hidden cut only after exact outfit approval.

### CHR-SAGE-CONT-001 — continuity sheet

- Stable source-linked identity and proportion descriptors.
- Must-preserve traits, allowed styling variation, and prohibited drift.
- Approved transformation relationship between runway and resort.
- Known source contradictions and how review resolves them.

## Allowed variation

- Expression within approved expression-sheet range.
- Runway-to-resort wardrobe, light, and environment transformation at the hidden cut.
- Hair motion consistent with the approved hair sheet and environmental force.
- Pose/gait phase that remains physically adjacent to neighboring frames.
- Minor grade/exposure adjustments that do not change skin tone or features.

## Prohibited drift

- Face averaging, face swap artifacts, mixed apparent age, eye-color changes, changed hairline, or altered distinguishing features.
- Height, limb, shoulder, waist, hand, foot, or head-to-body changes between frames.
- Skin-lightening/darkening caused by inconsistent grading rather than physical lighting.
- Unapproved makeup, tattoos, jewelry, logos, text, or wardrobe details.
- Extra/missing fingers, teeth artifacts, fused limbs, floating fabric, heel/floor mismatch, or impossible gait.
- Sudden screen-direction reversal or teleporting subject scale.

## Review protocol

Review identity before style:

1. Compare output at matched crop against at least three operation-authorized selected private sources.
2. Review front/three-quarter/profile separately; a strong front view does not approve an unseen profile.
3. Review full-body proportion on a neutral grid.
4. Review hands, feet, teeth, ears, hairline, jewelry, and garment edges at 100%.
5. Compare adjacent frames side by side and as a rapid A/B loop.
6. Record each defect in the manifest; do not hide it in chat history.
7. Sage or the designated likeness approver must approve the exact file/version.
