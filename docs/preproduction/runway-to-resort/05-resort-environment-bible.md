# Resort Environment Bible

Status: PROPOSED. The resort must be premium, tropical, and architecturally plausible without imitating a named property or making a partnership claim.

## Design intent

A bright, warm hospitality environment that reads immediately after the near-black transition: restrained resort architecture, a believable walking path, water and horizon context, selective tropical foliage, and enough quiet negative space for portfolio copy. Luxury comes from material quality, proportion, and light—not decorative excess.

Canonical environment ID: ENV-RESORT-001

## Fixed geometry and anchors

- A walkable path connects the transition emergence area to the final settle mark.
- Camera axis and subject screen direction continue from the runway.
- Horizon, waterline, primary building edge, path curve, major palm, pool/beach boundary, and sun direction are locked.
- Architecture obeys plausible structure, drainage, stairs/railings, openings, and material transitions.
- Foliage does not teleport or grow between frames.
- Decorative objects are excluded unless they support scale, route, or hospitality context.

## Material and object system

- Architecture: warm mineral plaster, pale stone, restrained timber, and clean shadow reveals.
- Paving: slip-resistant pale stone with coherent joints and contact shadows.
- Sand: natural warm neutral; no impossible footprints before subject path.
- Water: physically coherent horizon/reflection/refraction; no duplicated edges.
- Foliage: selected palms and coastal planting with consistent species/silhouette.
- Furniture/objects: minimal and structurally placed; no brand marks, signage, text, flags, or fantasy décor.
- The environment does not imply Sage has worked with or been hosted by a particular real resort.

## Lighting states

### LIGHT-RESORT-001 — warm emergence

- Begins from the same near-black level and edge direction as TRANS-OCCLUSION-001.
- A warm light leak reveals only enough geometry to establish physical continuation.
- Subject and camera movement remain continuous; no dissolve or teleport.

### LIGHT-RESORT-002 — full reveal

- Warm daylight with controlled highlight rolloff on skin, ivory wardrobe, stone, water, and sky.
- Sun direction, shadows, reflections, and foliage response align.
- Face remains source-accurate; “tropical” does not justify orange skin or teal-water overgrading.

### LIGHT-RESORT-003 — settled resolve

- Same time of day and sun direction with a slightly calmer exposure/grade.
- Final subject, CTA region, and hospitality anchors remain readable.
- Frame must work as a static poster.

## Camera contract

- Continue runway screen direction across the cut.
- Match perceived camera height at KF-04, then allow only an approved subtle settle.
- Start with the same lens family as runway or a clearly motivated, hidden-cut-compatible shift; record exact value.
- Preserve horizon and architectural verticals.
- Avoid drone, impossible crane, extreme wide, fisheye, or “infinite resort” geometry.
- Shutter/blur at KF-04 matches the departing black edge; KF-05/06 return to controlled clarity.
- Desktop and mobile use intentionally selected camera distances in the same world.

## Subject marks

- Mark D / KF-04: matched emergence area immediately beyond the hidden cut.
- Mark E / KF-05: reveal path with full resort context.
- Mark F / KF-06: settled final composition or glance-back mark.

Mark distances, subject-height percentage, foot position, gaze direction, and path relationship are recorded after the approved body/gait sheets.

## Desktop composition

- 16:9 with an open but specific resort vista.
- Keep horizon, subject, path, and primary architectural anchor inside 5% action safety.
- Reserve an owner-approved support-copy region and a stable final CTA region.
- Avoid placing high-contrast palm fronds or roof edges through the face.

## Mobile composition

- 9:16 authored separately with a stronger near-to-far path and controlled vertical architecture/foliage.
- Keep subject readable without shrinking to a decorative figure.
- Protect top browser-chrome, side action safety, and lower CTA/gesture clearance.
- Horizon and water remain evident without sacrificing full-body proportion.

## Review checklist

- Same horizon, waterline, path, building edge, palm anchors, sun direction, and time of day.
- Plausible architecture, materials, contact shadows, reflections, and subject scale.
- No real-property claims, logos, text, watermarks, or brand-coded décor.
- No wardrobe translucency or color contamination.
- Continuous screen direction and gait from KF-03/04 through KF-06.
- Final composition independently succeeds as poster and reduced-motion fallback.
- Desktop/mobile remain the same environment with deliberate framing.
